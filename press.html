The one big idea
When every competitor has the same AI, efficiency becomes the baseline — not the edge. The strongest margins will belong to the brands whose authentic experience can't be copied with a prompt. In an AI-saturated future, authenticity is the scarce asset — and scarcity is where the highest margins live.

AI will create bigger margins in the near term by automating work, reducing costs, and helping businesses move faster. But that advantage will not last. When every competitor has access to the same AI tools and can automate the same processes, efficiency will become the baseline — not the differentiator.

The businesses with the strongest margins will be the ones that use AI to amplify a unique, authentic brand experience that cannot be copied with a prompt. Technology should remove friction behind the scenes so a company can become more human, more memorable, and more emotionally distinct in front of the customer.

I see this in the services I'm building — AI guest experiences, operational automation, brand storytelling, and direct customer relationships: the automation creates capacity, but the brand experience creates preference and pricing power.

— Marco Cepeda, The Night Shift

Bio

Marco Cepeda runs Pier Walk on Deerfield Beach, a 20-unit beachfront property whose day-to-day operations — pricing, guest messaging, and marketing — run on a custom AI system he built himself.

After nearly burning out doing it all by hand, he rebuilt the entire operation as a stack of AI agents that quietly handle the repetitive work overnight. The Night Shift is how he now brings that same system — and the branding and storytelling that make a property stand out — to other independent operators who don't have an enterprise budget or a technical team.

He works with vacation rentals, boutique hotels, and local hospitality businesses that have a genuine story to tell and want the tools big brands use, without the big-brand price tag.
